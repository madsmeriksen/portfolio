Bachelor project

Description of project:
In the project, we explored if tangible user interfaces can improve the shopping experience in retail stores. To explore our reseach question a prototype was built user surveys was conducted in Sportmaster. Customers were then asked about the: interaction with the products, the information displayed on the screen and if the concept would improve their shopping experience.

Results:
Our results showed that using tangible user interfaces could make a positive difference for customers but also that it requires work before our concept can be seen as an integrated part of a retail shop and used succesfully by their customers.

Master thesis project

Description of project:
In this master thesis, we propose CapFloor, an interactive luminous floor consisting of LED-panels and a transparent capacitive sensing surface for tracking users in proximity or in contact. Before reaching our final prototype, we have been through an iterative design process. This process included, contextual inquiries and exploration of different sensing technologies. Additionally, we investigated the core difficulties that exists when using capacitive sensing in a sensing surface that is placed directly on an LED-floor. Finally, we show an example of how CapFloor can be used to guide and motivate users during their workout.

Results:
This thesis contributes to the HCI community with an alternative approach to create interactive luminous floors. Through knowledge obtained during the design process, we contribute with:
    An example of using interactive luminous floors for training guidance and motivation
    An exploration of capacitive sensing.
    An overview of the advantages and limitations concerning a selection of technologies applied in interactive floors.

Interactive spaces

Description of project:
In the course Interactive Spaces we were given the assignment to create an interactive prototype for The Botanical Garden in Aarhus. Throughout our design process, we focus on and work with the tropical space within The Botanical Garden. In the beginning of the course, we were on a guided tour where Kamma and Karen, two employes presented some of their ideas and problems linked to the exhibition at The Botanical Garden. One of the problems revealed through the tour with Kamma and Karen was how to pass on information about the plants and trees in a more engaging and understandable way. By using the literature from the course and feedback from both the teachers and Kamma and Karen we have created a final concept.

Results:
The final concept contains a mobile application and physical instalments that makes an engaging and educational learning experience for children and their families. Through the concept, we attempt to make visitors explore the tropical space and seek information about plants and how the nature relates to humans. We do this without disturbing the current aesthetically experience that exists in the space.

Shape changing interfaces

Description of project:
In this project, we explored how transitions, light, interaction, shape-change and materials can create a metaphor that cause feelings and change in behaviour within people. Through experiments and the making of prototypes, explored each of these design parameters and how they impact the hedonic goals.

Results:
Our research contributed with additional knowledge to the creation of imagined physics through the use of shape-changing interfaces. It showed us how the interdependency of movements, materials used, light, sound created and type of shape-change can contribute to a hedonic and behavioural change. Furthermore, the research conveyed some problems that emerged when putting all the design parameters together when building a strong metaphor and how a small change in the choice of a material can cause relaxation instead of frustration and anger.
